Explore Kerala

Caressed by Arabian Sea and Western Ghats,Kerala has 14 separate districts, each famous for its own set of unique destinations and features. Following is a list of that can be used to gain information on each individual district based on your preference

Alappuzha

Known as the Venice of the East, Alappuzha has a lot to offer. From excellent beaches and backwaters to beautiful temples and famous lighthouse, this destination will never fail to impress you in terms of natural beauty and picturesque locations. Welcome to Alappey.

Ernankulam

Kochi has a quaint ambience and the colonial buildings, telling the tales of centuries-old cultural and commercial interaction with the colonial powers of the West, makes a big impression.

Idukki

Mist laden hills, enchanting valleys, gushing streams, cascades, rolling grasslands, sprawling plantations, colonial bungalows, wildlife and many more makes Idukki, a unique destination for travellers.

Kannur

Welcome to the Land of Looms & Lores.

Kasaragod

The land of seven languages and several cultures, welcome to Kasaragod.

Kollam

Let the lazy Ashtamudi backwaters set the pace to your day and village folktales flow to your heart. Let the misty mountains awake you with whispered tales of long-forgotten travellers.Kollam isn't just a destination, it's a saga of experiences!

Kottayam

A fine blend of tradition, natural beauty and progress, this place has much to offer to the varying tastes of each discerning traveller. Discover Kottayam.

Kozhikode

The whiff of history continues to permeate the lanes, bazaars and business hubs of Kozhikode. Lush green countryside, serene beaches, historic sites, wildlife sanctuaries, rivers and hills make Kozhikode a popular destination.

Malapuram

Historic monuments, natural attractions, ritual art forms, ethnic cuisine and age-old traditions make Malappuram a unique destination for travelers.

Pathanamthitta

Known as the 'Headquarters of Pilgrimage Tourism',of the Kerala State. Situated near the Western Ghats and bordered by the hills, Pathanamthitta district is a treat to eyes with its vast unending stretches of forests, rivers and rural landscapes.

Palakkad

Celebrated as the Granary of Kerala, Palakkad is a vast stretch of fertile plains interspersed with hills, rivers, mountain streams and forests.

Thiruvananthpuram

This "Evergreen City of India" is located in the southwestern tip of Indian mainland and lies straddled by the Arabian Sea in the west and the magnificent highlands of Western Ghats in the east. The capital city and cultural hub of God's Own Country.

Thrissur

The cultural capital of Kerala, is a hotbed of folk art performances and heritage tours that will give you an in depth look into some of the grandest traditions of the State.

Wayanad

Wayanad, the green paradise is nestled among the mountains of the Western Ghats, forming the border world of the greener part of Kerala. Clean and pristine, enchanting and hypnotizing, this land is filled with history and culture.
